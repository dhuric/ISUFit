Workout Ideas

Discover new exercises to enhance your fitness journey and achieve your personal health goals.

Bench Press primarily targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps.

  • Start by lying flat on a bench with your feet firmly planted on the ground.
  • Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, with your hands facing forward.
  • Lower the barbell towards your chest while keeping your elbows tucked in.
  • Press the barbell back up to the starting position, fully extending your arms.
  • Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps, adjusting the weight to challenge your muscles.

Deadlift are a compound exercise that works multiple muscle groups, including the hamstrings, glutes, lower back, and forearms.

  • Stand with your feet hip-width apart, toes under the barbell.
  • Bend your knees and grip the barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Keep your back flat and chest up as you lift the barbell by extending your hips and knees simultaneously.
  • Stand tall with the barbell, then lower it back down with control.
  • Perform 3-4 sets of 5-8 reps, ensuring proper form and gradually increasing the weight as you progress.

Squat target the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and lower back.

  • Begin with the barbell racked on your shoulders, feet shoulder-width apart or slightly wider.
  • Engage your core, keep your chest up, and lower your body by bending at the knees and hips.
  • Descend until your thighs are parallel to the ground or slightly below, ensuring your knees track over your toes.
  • Drive through your heels to return to the starting position, fully extending your hips and knees.
  • Aim for 3-4 sets of 8-10 reps, adjusting the weight as needed to maintain proper form and intensity.
